# XBeton — Product Visual Reference & AI Prompt Kit

The purpose of this kit is to keep every AI-generated image and video **consistent with the real product** — correct size, proportions, color, texture, and features. Generative tools don't know XBeton's product; left alone they invent grey cinder blocks, brick, or rough aggregate concrete. This document gives you (and any tool) one locked source of truth, ready-to-paste prompts, and a workflow for feeding in real reference photos.

**How to use it:** keep the [Product Truth Sheet](#1-product-truth-sheet) open as your fact reference, paste the [Locked Product Block](#the-locked-product-block-paste-into-every-prompt) into every image/video prompt, and follow the [Reference-Image Workflow](#4-reference-image-workflow-the-real-consistency-lever) for anything that has to be exact.

## 1. Product Truth Sheet

### What XBeton actually is
Autoclaved Aerated Concrete (AAC) — a lightweight precast building material made from sand, cement, lime, water, and a touch of aluminum powder, cured under high-pressure steam. XBeton's version adds a **nano-graphene coating/admixture (Bond & Shield)**. It is one-third the weight of traditional concrete/CMU.

### Catalog at a glance — 33 SKUs across 3 families
The full product catalog totals **33 items**:

- **Blocks — 9 SKUs:** Solid ×3, Cored ×3, U-Block ×3 (all 8"×24" face, 6/8/12" thick).
- **Panels — 17 SKUs:** Wall ×6, Roof/Floor ×5, Cladding/Fence ×6 (all 24" wide).
- **Bond & Shield — 7 SKUs:** XBOND group ×4 (XBASE dose pack + 3 accessories), XSHIELD group ×3 (XRENDER, XSEAL, XFINISH).

### Appearance — the look that must stay consistent
*(Confirmed against XBeton's official dimension diagrams and brochures — saved in `Reference Assets/`.)*
- **Color:** ranges from pale concrete-grey to near-white. Matte, never glossy. The lighter, cleaner pieces read almost chalk-white; cored/working blocks read pale grey with visible fine speckle. (Never the dark charcoal-grey of a cinder block.)
- **Surface texture:** finely porous — a uniform field of tiny air pockets, like a dense sponge or aerated chocolate, *not* coarse gravel/aggregate. Smooth to the eye from a few feet away, lightly pitted/speckled up close.
- **Edges & faces:** precise, clean, factory-cut rectangular edges. Flat, even faces. Crisp 90° corners. **Panels and blocks have a tongue-and-groove (shiplap) profile along their long edges** — a stepped interlock for assembly. Worth showing on edge-on shots.
- **Form factor:** large, clean rectangular blocks and big flat panels — modern, engineered, precast. Reads "high-tech building system," not "old masonry."

### Brand identity (keep consistent across all assets)
- **Logo:** "XBETON" wordmark — blue letters with a red/blue swoosh "X", inside a red rounded-rectangle outline, ™ mark. Never let AI redraw it; overlay the real logo in editing instead.
- **Brand colors:** deep blue + red on white/light grey; dark charcoal backgrounds for premium product shots.
- **Taglines in use:** "Stronger. Lighter. Faster. Safer." · "Next-Generation AAC with NANO Technology" · "Innovation in Every Cell" · "Fireproof up to 6,500°F."
- **Trust badges:** NET ZERO, UL, ICC-ES, ASTM, TMS 402/602, ACI.
- **Contact (for asset footers):** www.xbeton.com · info@xbeton.com · 855-2XBETON. Beton Holdings LLC, DBA XBeton.

### Blocks — dimensions & proportions
All blocks share an **8" × 24" face** (≈200 × 600 mm). The 1:3 face proportion is the single most important visual cue — XBeton blocks are long and low, roughly the length of three stacked, **not** square cinder blocks.

| Block type | Face | Thicknesses | Distinguishing feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Solid (AAC-4)** | 8" × 24" | 6", 8", 12" | Plain solid rectangular block |
| **Cored (AAC-4)** | 8" × 24" | 6", 8", 12" | **One round vertical core hole** through the top face (for rebar/conduit) — a single round hole, not the twin oval cells of a cinder block |
| **U-Block (AAC-4)** | 8" × 24" | 6", 8", 12" | U-shaped channel along the top (bond beams/lintels) |

- Density ~31–37 pcf; **70% lighter than concrete**.
- Compressive strength 512–1280 PSI; R-22 to R-41 assemblies; up to 6h fire rating with nano-graphene.

### Panels — dimensions & proportions
All panels are **24" wide**. Length and thickness vary by type. Panels span up to **19'6"**.

| Panel type | Face (W × L) | Thicknesses | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Wall** | 24" × 96"–236" | 8", 10", 12" | Load-bearing walls |
| **Roof / Floor** | 24" × 144"–236" | 8", 10", 12" | Horizontal spans (ring-anchor system) |
| **Cladding / Fence** | 24" × 48"–120" | 2", 3", 4" | Facade, perimeter, fencing (thin) |

- 1/5 the weight of concrete; reinforced with rebar + ring-anchor steel cleats; crane-set on site.

### Bond & Shield — appearance & product line
A separate visual category from the blocks/panels: these are **graphene dose packs (pre-measured powder systems sold in branded bags/sacks)** plus a few jobsite accessories. Powder is mixed on-site.

- **Appearance:** **stand-up pouch bags** (resealable foil/poly), white body with a colored top band and the XBETON logo, GHS hazard pictograms and a QR code on the front. Palletized for jobsite delivery. Grey/graphene-dark powder when shown loose.
- **The 7 catalog SKUs (source of truth):**
  - **XSHIELD — coatings (3):** XRENDER (graphene base coat / render), XSEAL (joint sealant + WRB / vapor control), XFINISH (exterior + interior finish coat).
  - **XBOND — 1 dose pack + 3 hardware accessories (4):** XBASE (leveling + structural repair) + AAC-compatible anchor bolt, screw anchor, and joint-reinforcement mesh tape.
- **About the bag poster (THINSET / REPAIR / STUCCO / EXTERIOR & INTERIOR FINISH COAT):** these are **plain-language usage labels** on a consumer education graphic — they help customers understand *what each product is for*, and are **not** additional SKUs. Color-coded by top band (THINSET black, REPAIR blue, STUCCO green, EXTERIOR brown). The dose-pack/coating products behind them are the XBASE / XRENDER / XSEAL / XFINISH SKUs above. (Interior Finish Coat is noted as the only non-graphene formula.)
- **Visual note for AI:** show as **stand-up pouch bags with a colored top band**, not wet concrete, not paint cans, not paper sacks. Always drive the bag art from a real reference image — AI mangles the logo, the GHS pictograms, and the label text every time.

### Scale anchors (how to make size read correctly in an image)
AI loses scale constantly. Always give it a reference object:
- A **worker** hand-carrying a single block one-handed (sells "lightweight" + size).
- A **standard wood pallet** stacked with blocks (60–120 per pallet).
- A **crane or two workers** setting a full-height wall panel (sells the long-span story).
- A **standard door or window** in a wall assembly for architectural scale.

### Anti-patterns — what it must NEVER look like
Put these in your negative prompt every time:
> grey cinder block, CMU, two oval holes, hollow concrete block, red brick, rough gravel/aggregate concrete, exposed stones, cracked or crumbling, wet/glossy concrete, rebar sticking out randomly, dark charcoal color, rounded edges, hand-made/rustic, foam insulation board, drywall.

### Video glitch-reduction settings (fixes the weirdness you saw in Veo/Gemini)
The artifacts you hit come from too much motion and the model re-inventing the product frame to frame. To minimize them:
- **Start from a still image** you already approved (image-to-video) — this locks the look. This is the #1 fix.
- **Keep clips short:** 5–8 seconds. Drift compounds over time.
- **Slow, simple camera moves only** — locked, slow dolly, or slow rotation. Avoid fast pans, zooms, and anything with people's hands doing fast detailed work (where morphing shows worst).
- **Avoid close-up fast motion on faces/text/logos** — these warp first.
- **Generate 3–4 takes and cut** — treat outputs as raw b-roll, edit the clean seconds together.

## 4. Reference-Image Workflow (the real consistency lever)

Prompts alone won't perfectly hold size and features — **feeding in real reference images will.** This is what makes outputs look like *your* product instead of a generic block.

### Step 1 — Build a canonical reference set (one time)
**You already have a strong starting set** in `02 Projects/XBeton/Reference Assets/`: the two official brochures, plus XBeton's own dimension diagrams for every block/panel (solid, cored, U-block, wall, floor/roof, cladding/fence) and the Bond & Shield product poster. Use those as your baseline references now; add real plant photos as they come in. Aim for clean, real images covering:
1. Single solid block, straight-on face (shows 8×24 proportion).
2. Single block, 45° angle (shows thickness + edges).
3. Cored block from the top (shows cores).
4. U-block (shows channel).
5. Close-up of the surface texture.
6. A finished wall / installed assembly.
7. A panel being craned (scale).
8. A pallet of blocks (scale + packaging).
9. A Bond & Shield bag/sack (correct branding + label text).
10. The accessories — anchor bolt, screw anchor, mesh tape (so AI doesn't invent them).

Your website images and product brochure are a good starting source. Real photos beat AI references.

### Step 2 — Feed references into each tool
- **ChatGPT (GPT-image):** attach 1–3 reference photos in the chat and say *"Match the product in these reference images exactly — same color, texture, proportions, and edges."* Then give the scene prompt + Locked Product Block.
- **Gemini (Imagen / Nano-Banana editing):** upload the reference and use its image-editing/reference mode to place the same product into a new scene; strong for keeping a subject consistent across shots.
- **Video (Veo / Gemini video):** generate or pick a still you approve, then use **image-to-video** so the first frame is your locked product. Don't text-prompt video from scratch when consistency matters.

### Step 3 — Lock and reuse
- When a tool nails the look, **save that image as a new reference** and reuse it as the seed for the next ones. Consistency snowballs.
- If the tool supports **seeds**, note the seed of a good result and reuse it for sibling shots.
- Keep a small **approved library** (hero block, wall, panel, cladding) so every future post pulls from the same visual DNA.

## 5. Tool Recommendation (given your Pro accounts)

Model capabilities shift fast, so treat this as a starting split rather than a permanent verdict — but based on current strengths and what you already observed:

| Need | Recommended | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Product-accurate still images** | **ChatGPT / GPT-image** | You already found chat "doing great." Best at following detailed written specs and reference photos; strong text/logo handling for labeled product shots. |
| **Placing the same product into many scenes** | **Gemini (image editing/reference)** | Strong subject-consistency and reference-image editing for spinning one approved block into many backdrops. |
| **Video / motion b-roll** | **Veo / Gemini video — but image-to-video only** | Best realism (the "natural and realism factors" you noted). The glitches come from text-to-video and too much motion; drive it from an approved still and keep clips short. |

**Practical division of labor:** lock the hero stills in ChatGPT → use those approved stills as the reference/first frame in Gemini and Veo → keep everything anchored to the same saved reference set. That single habit — *everything traces back to one approved image* — is what delivers the consistency you're after.
