# XBeton — Plant Photo & Video Shot List

A field checklist for a plant/jobsite shoot. It captures two things at once:
1. **Clean product reference frames** — the canonical set that locks AI image/video to the real product (pairs with `[C] Product Visual Reference & AI Prompt Kit.md`).
2. **Real marketing assets** — process, people, and scale shots you can post directly and use as b-roll.

Print this or pull it up on a phone at the plant. Tick boxes as you go.

---

## Before you start — 5-minute setup

- **Camera:** a recent phone is fine. Shoot in the **highest resolution**, HDR off for product shots, and **lock focus/exposure** by tapping and holding on the product.
- **Two backdrops:**
  - *Product shots* → a clean, uncluttered background (a roll of seamless paper, a clean wall, or a plain pallet in open shade). Consistency matters more than fanciness — use the **same backdrop and lighting** for every product so the set looks like a family.
  - *Process/people shots* → the real plant floor, in context.
- **Light:** soft, even, indirect daylight is best (open shade or an overcast spot near a big door). **Avoid harsh midday sun** and mixed colored factory lighting — they wreck color accuracy on pale AAC.
- **Always include a scale cue** in at least one frame of each item: a hand, a tape measure, a worker, a pallet, or a door.
- **Shoot each setup as a short burst** and grab **5–10 sec of video** while you're there (slow pan) — free b-roll.
- **Wipe dust** off product faces; pale AAC shows every smudge.

## TRACK A — Product reference frames (clean, neutral background)

Each product needs the same core angles: **face-on** (proportion), **45°** (thickness + edges), and a **macro** (texture). These are what make AI outputs match the real thing.

### Blocks — 8" × 24" face (shoot the 8" thickness; note thickness range 6"–12")

**Solid block**
- [ ] Face-on, straight (shows the 8×24, long-low 1:3 proportion) — `XB_block_solid_face`
- [ ] 45° hero angle (shows thickness + crisp edges) — `XB_block_solid_45`
- [ ] Edge-on, close (shows the **tongue-and-groove profile** on the long edge) — `XB_block_solid_edge`

**Cored block**
- [ ] Top-down (shows the **single round core hole** clearly — not twin cinder-block cells) — `XB_block_cored_top`
- [ ] 45° angle showing core + thickness — `XB_block_cored_45`

**U-block**
- [ ] 45° angle showing the **U-channel** down the top — `XB_block_ublock_45`
- [ ] End-on, into the channel — `XB_block_ublock_end`

**Surface texture (do once, applies to all)**
- [ ] Extreme macro of a clean face — the fine uniform air-pocket speckle, smooth-but-pitted — `XB_texture_macro`

### Panels — 24" wide (tongue-and-groove long edges)

**Wall panel (8"–12" thick, 96"–236" long)**
- [ ] Full length, slight angle (conveys the long span) — `XB_panel_wall_full`
- [ ] Edge-on close-up (thickness + T&G interlock) — `XB_panel_wall_edge`

**Floor / Roof panel (8"–12" thick, 144"–236" long)**
- [ ] Full length, slight angle — `XB_panel_floor_full`
- [ ] End showing thickness + ring-anchor/reinforcement detail if visible — `XB_panel_floor_end`

**Cladding / Fence panel (thin, 2"–4" thick, 48"–120")**
- [ ] Face-on (clean facade look) — `XB_panel_cladding_face`
- [ ] Edge-on (shows how thin it is vs. the structural panels) — `XB_panel_cladding_edge`

### Bond & Shield — the 7 SKUs

**Dose-pack / coating bags (stand-up pouches, colored top band, logo + GHS icons + QR)**
- [ ] XBASE bag, front straight-on — `XB_bond_xbase_bag_front`
- [ ] XRENDER bag, front — `XB_shld_xrender_bag_front`
- [ ] XSEAL bag, front — `XB_shld_xseal_bag_front`
- [ ] XFINISH bag, front — `XB_shld_xfinish_bag_front`
- [ ] Group shot — all bags lined up on a pallet (the "system" hero) — `XB_bond_group`
- [ ] One bag at 45° (shows it's a pouch, not a paper sack) — `XB_bond_bag_45`

**Hardware accessories (small parts — shoot on clean background with a coin/hand for scale)**
- [ ] AAC anchor bolt — `XB_bond_anchor`
- [ ] AAC screw anchor — `XB_bond_screw`
- [ ] Joint-reinforcement mesh tape (roll + unrolled) — `XB_bond_mesh`

## TRACK B — Marketing & b-roll (real plant context)

These sell the story: lightweight, fast, clean, high-tech. Get people in frame (with safety gear + permission).

### Scale & "lightweight" proof
- [ ] Worker carrying a full block **one-handed** (the money shot for "70% lighter") — photo + video
- [ ] One worker lifting a panel that would take a crew in concrete
- [ ] Block next to a standard CMU/cinder block for size+weight contrast
- [ ] Full pallet of blocks with a person beside it for scale

### Manufacturing / process (the "innovation in every cell" story)
- [ ] Wide shot of the plant floor / production line
- [ ] AAC being **cut to size** (wire-cutting, if applicable) — great motion b-roll
- [ ] Autoclave / curing area (the high-tech proof)
- [ ] Close-up of the nano-graphene admixture / Bond & Shield being mixed or applied
- [ ] Stacks of finished product, palletized and wrapped
- [ ] Quality check / measuring a block (precision story)

### Installation / assembly
- [ ] Workers laying a block wall with thin-bed mortar (trowel detail)
- [ ] A doorway or window opening in a partial wall (architectural scale)
- [ ] Crane setting a wall panel into place
- [ ] Cutting/shaping a block on-site with standard tools (ease-of-use story)
- [ ] Finished/rendered wall with XRENDER + XFINISH applied

### Hero / brand
- [ ] A clean, finished AAC wall in good light (cover-image quality)
- [ ] Tight detail of the surface texture on an installed wall
- [ ] Team/plant pride shot (people + branded gear or signage)

### Video b-roll specifics (for the social campaign)
- [ ] 5–10 sec **slow pans** across: the line, a pallet stack, a wall being built
- [ ] A **block being carried** (motion sells the weight)
- [ ] Cutting/mixing in motion
- *Keep clips steady and slow — these become the realistic base frames for any AI video, per the prompt kit.*

---

## Priority if time is short (shoot these first)
1. Solid block — face-on + 45° + texture macro.
2. Cored block — top-down (the single core).
3. Worker carrying a block one-handed (lightweight proof).
4. Bond & Shield group shot on a pallet.
5. One finished wall in good light.

These five alone unlock the hero stills *and* give the social launch its first real assets.
