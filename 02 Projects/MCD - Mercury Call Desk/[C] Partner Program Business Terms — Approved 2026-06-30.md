# MCD Partner Program Business Terms — Approved 2026-06-30

## 1. Company and program identity

```txt
Contracting company: Charter Oaks Assets, Inc. d/b/a Mercury Call Desk ("MCD")
Authorized company signer: Hamilton Pinto Jr., Manager and Authorized Signatory
Business address: 231 E Alessandro Blvd A-208, Riverside, CA 92508
Program name: Independent Sales Partner Program
Partner status: commission-only 1099 Sales Partner / Independent Contractor
Customer-facing role title: Account Executive, Sales Representative, or Sales Agent
Future pathway: qualifying partners may later be considered for a separate reseller agreement
```

This program is a business-in-a-box entry pathway: MCD supplies approved brand assets, CRM/portal tools, training, customer-contract handling, implementation support, billing, accounting, and Level 2 escalation. Partners independently control their schedules, locations, prospecting approach, and non-competing business activity. MCD does not promise income, results, or reseller status.

> **Legal guardrail:** These are approved business terms, not a legal determination of worker classification. The final agreement must receive California attorney review before release or signature.

## 2. Mandatory onboarding and access gate

No paperwork, no system access, and no exceptions. Before Hamilton countersigns and grants active MiniCRM/GHL access, every Partner must complete:

1. Complete CRM profile, including verified personal email, mobile number, mailing address, and required emergency/contact information.
2. IRS Form W-9.
3. Signed Sales Partner Agreement.
4. Signed Confidentiality and Intellectual Property Agreement.
5. CRM training/check-in completion.

The system of record must retain the signed PDF, agreement version, timestamp, signer IP/audit trail, countersignature, W-9, profile-completion record, and certification/training completion.

## 3. Portal and access model

- Each Partner receives a dedicated backend GHL user plus a MiniCRM role so MCD can securely store Partner profile, agreement, attribution, commission, and client-service data.
- During the active relationship, operational/internal communications may use the MCD-provided company email. Formal legal or civil notices go to the Partner's verified personal email.
- On departure, company email and active-user access end immediately.
- A former Partner in good standing may receive a restricted, read-only CRM role only for retained clients, commission dashboard, and meeting notes. That role has no OpenPool, Shark Tank, directory, lead, export, workflow, billing, settings, or administrative access, and ends 30 days after the final commission payout clears.
- Partner and agreement records are retained for seven years.

## 4. Partner authority and progression

### Standard Sales Partner

- May prospect independently, use approved branding/collateral, explain approved published pricing, and book qualified demos after CRM training.
- Must use published standard prices. May not independently discount, waive setup fees, alter scope, create customer contracts, bind MCD, or change any terms.
- May sell non-competing products or services through their independent business. MCD products/services may not be displaced or competed against.

### Advanced Partner / Manager pathway

- Advanced authority is granted only by MCD after certification and written approval.
- May run demos end-to-end, close authorized deals, and customize customer prices above MCD's approved floor.
- No custom price may fall below MCD's minimum percentage margin above published partner wholesale cost plus payment-processing fees.
- Any discount, setup-fee waiver, nonstandard scope, or exception requires explicit manager approval before a contract is generated.

### Reseller pathway

- This Sales Partner Agreement grants no franchise, territory, ownership interest, reseller status, exclusive sales right, or authority to sublicense MCD.
- A reseller relationship, if offered, requires a separate written agreement.

## 5. Lead attribution, protection, and reassignment

- All leads, prospects, customers, notes, recordings, meetings, proposals, and CRM records are Company records and must remain in approved systems.
- A lead exists when entered in the CRM. A non-referral cold lead is not protected merely by entry; it becomes protected only after documented two-way contact is recorded in the CRM.
- A self-sourced referral is protected immediately upon CRM entry when the Partner documents the source accurately.
- A Partner who books the qualified demo is credited for the sale only when the customer closes and MCD receives cleared payment.
- There are no standard MCD-administered split commissions. Partners may make private arrangements among themselves, but MCD pays the credited Partner of record unless management documents a different allocation in advance.
- **OpenPool:** a cold lead with no demo booked after 45 days becomes open to all authorized Partners. The original Partner loses the lead.
- **Shark Tank:** a pitched lead that received contract pricing and stalled may be assigned only to top-tier closers/managers. The original Partner loses the lead.
- If another Partner re-engages, rebooks, closes, and receives payment from a reassigned lead, that Partner receives the Sales Partner share. A former booking Partner has no commission on a deal that never closed.
- MCD may move any account to House for client protection, noncompliance, abandonment, or operational necessity. Referral-source attribution does not override MCD's right to protect the client and reassign servicing.

## 6. Pricing, setup fees, commission, and payout

### Net Commissionable Profit

```txt
Net Commissionable Profit = collected retail revenue
                          − published partner wholesale cost
                          − actual Stripe/payment-processing fee
```

- MCD retains 50% of Net Commissionable Profit; the credited Sales Partner receives 50%.
- Sales tax is not commissionable revenue.
- All customer payments are processed through MCD.
- Refunds, credits, discounts, package adjustments, and customer-service commitments are controlled by MCD and the customer agreement.

### Setup fees

- Setup fees are scope-based and may be waived only with explicit manager approval, including for approved annual commitments.
- A setup fee must first cover the customer's first-month MCD wholesale cost and payment-processing fees. The remaining setup profit is split 50/50 between MCD and the credited Partner.
- Minimum setup-profit targets before the 50/50 split:

| Package | Minimum setup-profit target |
|---|---:|
| Starter | $1,000 |
| Growth | $1,500 |
| Pro | $3,000 |
| Enterprise | $5,000 |

### Payout timing

- New-account first-payment and setup-fee commissions: paid no later than 30 days after cleared funds and the documented launch checklist confirms the applicable launch phase is complete.
- Established-account recurring payments and unchanged renewals: paid within 15 days after cleared funds.
- A package, scope, pricing, or contract-structure change is treated as a new contract for commission timing and calculation.
- Annual contracts renew under the established-account rule only while the package/scope remains unchanged.

### Cancellation, chargebacks, and disputes

- Month-to-month plans may have a 10-day cancellation window beginning on the payment-cleared date, subject to the customer agreement.
- Setup fees are nonrefundable; service fees are nonrefundable once the service month begins, except as required by law or expressly approved by MCD.
- Annual plans are use-it-or-lose-it and nonrefundable except as required by law or expressly approved by MCD.
- In the rare event of a refund, chargeback, payment reversal, fraud, duplicate payout, or Partner negligence, the Partner is responsible for their related commission portion. MCD may hold or offset future commissions as reasonably needed to resolve the balance.
- The Partner has 10 days after notice to repay an established balance before MCD may offset future commissions.
- MCD must notify the Partner promptly through the system/company email and record the reason and calculation.
- Commission disputes must be submitted in writing within 30 days. CRM attribution and documented CRM notes are the controlling evidence; Hamilton audits disputes in good faith.

## 7. Client service, support, and residual eligibility

A Partner earns residual commissions only while the customer remains current and the Partner remains in good standing and services the account under these standards.

| Package | Phase 1 cadence | Ongoing cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | Biweekly during the initial phase (2 weeks to 3 months) | Monthly |
| Growth | Weekly during the initial phase | Biweekly or monthly, based on client preference |
| Pro | Weekly | Guaranteed one-hour weekly session |
| Enterprise | Weekly | Guaranteed one-hour weekly session |

- Meetings are generally scheduled through Google Meet for one hour, although routine check-ins may take only about 15 minutes once the account is stable.
- A documented meeting offer or client-declined/rescheduled meeting counts as compliant service activity.
- Clients may decline a session and wait for the next scheduled session.
- Clients use `support@mercurycalldesk.com` for support intake. MCD handles Level 1 intake and the Partner handles account-facing communication within their role; MCD/Hamilton handles Level 2 escalation.
- Standard target response time is 24 calendar hours. Pro and Enterprise setup/support matters receive priority handling and are generally addressed more quickly when feasible.
- An unexcused failure to respond to a client and escalate the need for three calendar days may justify immediate termination.
- A failure to attend to or confirm client account health for 60 days is a servicing-compliance violation. The first violation results in a warning; a second violation in a rolling 12-month period results in loss of the account to House. Material decisions and changes must be documented through CRM and e-signed acknowledgments when applicable.

## 8. Separation, retirement, and House Accounts

- MCD may terminate the relationship at will and without cause, subject to applicable law. Voluntary Partners are encouraged, but not required, to provide 14 days' notice.
- Immediate termination may result from fraud, customer theft, misrepresentation, legal/compliance violations, failure to honor opt-outs, data export or misuse, harassment, or brand misuse.
- On termination, active access and company email end immediately. Accounts remain Company records and transfer to House unless MCD grants a good-standing former Partner restricted access for selected clients.
- A good-standing departing Partner may select clients they will continue to service. Residuals continue only for selected clients that remain active/current and are documented as serviced in the CRM. Released or unserviced clients become House Accounts and future residuals end.
- Retiring Partners may propose an approved next-of-kin successor. The successor must register, complete onboarding/certification, use the CRM, and follow all MCD policies before servicing accounts. MCD may end retirement status, residuals, or succession arrangements in good faith for defined business reasons or client-protection needs.

## 9. Communications, DNC, brand, confidentiality, and data

- Personal social accounts and personal phones may be used for independent prospecting in good faith. Meaningful contact must be logged in the CRM.
- Customer/prospect email must be sent through the MCD CRM/company-email system. MCD has no active Partner phone/SMS program at this time.
- Any opt-out, DNC, unsubscribe, or "stop" received by any channel must be logged immediately. DNC means a total blackout from calls, SMS, sales email, marketing email, and social DMs. Transactional support, billing, security, and system-outage notices may continue where permitted.
- Partners may publicly identify themselves in outbound business activity as an MCD Account Executive, Sales Representative, or Sales Agent and use approved website/brand assets.
- Partners may not disclose internal pricing mechanics, wholesale costs, commission splits, vendor relationships, backend operations, lead/client data, scripts, or other nonpublic information.
- Specific client names, logos, screenshots, testimonials, or identifiable case studies require written MCD approval and written client approval. Generic, anonymized experience/metrics may be used in good faith.
- No lead/customer data may be exported, downloaded, copied to personal storage, or placed in unapproved spreadsheets, personal email, messaging apps, or devices.
- Any actual or suspected improper storage, export, screenshot, disclosure, or misuse must be reported within 24 hours. Accidental storage is handled case-by-case; intentional export, screenshots, sharing, or misuse may result in immediate termination.
- After departure, all local copies, screenshots, exports, and messages containing Company information must be deleted within 24 hours. MCD relies on the Partner's attestation and system records; no separate exit certificate is required.

## 10. Disputes and amendments

- Civil disputes: written notice, good-faith resolution attempt, then individual binding arbitration administered by JAMS in Riverside County, California, with virtual participation permitted.
- MCD and the Partner split JAMS filing, administration, and arbitrator fees equally unless applicable law, JAMS rules, or the arbitrator requires another allocation. Each Party pays its own attorney fees unless a law or final award provides otherwise.
- Suspected criminal conduct, fraud, theft, or data misuse may be referred to law enforcement. Requests for injunctions/equitable relief protecting confidential information or IP may be brought in court where permitted.
- MCD gives at least 30 days' notice for new industry, package, or consultation pricing structures. A shorter 10-day notice may apply before a new client's first payment when necessary for a new structure.
- As long as a client's contract pricing and structure do not change, the commission structure remains the same. If package, price, scope, or contract structure changes, commissions adjust under the updated contract terms.
