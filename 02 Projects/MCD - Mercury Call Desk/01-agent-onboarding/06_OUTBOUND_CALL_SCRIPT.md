# Approved Outbound Call Script

## The purpose of the first call

The purpose is to earn a short discovery conversation and, when qualified, book a no-cost demo. It is not to force a close or make technical promises.

## Ethical opener

> “Hi, this is [YOUR NAME] with Mercury Call Desk. I know this is an unexpected call. We help businesses reduce missed calls and handle more customer inquiries through an AI-assisted call desk. I wanted to see how your team handles calls when everyone is busy or the office is closed.”

## Continue the conversation

> “When a customer calls after hours or while the team is tied up, what usually happens?”

Listen. Then:

> “That is exactly the type of workflow we help evaluate. We can show you a short, no-cost demo of how AI-assisted call handling could route inquiries, capture leads, and support your team based on your workflow. Would it be useful to set aside 15 minutes with the person who oversees calls or operations?”

## Demo booking close

> “Great. To make the demo worthwhile, I would like to understand your current call process and make sure the right person is included. What day and time usually works best?”

## If they are busy

> “No problem. I will keep this brief. Is it better to reconnect [offer two specific windows], or should I send a short overview for the right person to review?”

## If they say they already have a phone system

> “That makes sense. Most businesses we speak with already have one. The question is whether it is consistently handling missed calls, after-hours inquiries, routing, and follow-up the way you want. A short demo can show whether there is any gap worth solving.”

## If they say “send information”

> “Absolutely. I will send a concise overview. To make it relevant, what is the biggest call-handling challenge you would want the information to address?”

Then log the answer, send approved collateral, and create the follow-up task.

## What not to say

- Do not use a false reason for calling.
- Do not say the platform guarantees revenue, savings, compliance, or staffing reductions.
- Do not claim the prospect is eligible, approved, or a perfect fit before discovery.
- Do not use unapproved discounts, payment terms, or implementation timelines.
- Do not pressure a prospect who asks to be removed from contact.
