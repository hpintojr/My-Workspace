# Recording Disclosure & Compliant Call Openers

> Recording-consent law varies by state (some require all-party consent). **Do not record a call without the approved disclosure and, where required, consent.** Confirm the policy with management/counsel before recording. Honor do-not-call/opt-out immediately and log it.

## Recording disclosure (use when a call may be recorded)
> "Quick note before we continue — this call may be recorded for quality and training. Is that okay with you?"

- If they say no: do not record; continue without recording (or end if required), and note it in the CRM.

## Compliant cold-call opener (approved)
> "Hi, this is [Your Name] with Mercury Call Desk. I know this is an unexpected call. We help businesses reduce missed calls and handle more customer inquiries with an AI-assisted call desk. I wanted to ask how your team handles calls when everyone's busy or the office is closed."

## Bridge to discovery
> "When a customer calls after hours or while the team is tied up, what usually happens?" (listen) → "That's exactly the kind of workflow we help evaluate. Could I show you a short, no-cost demo tailored to your setup — 15 minutes with whoever oversees calls?"

## Voicemail (approved)
> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] with Mercury Call Desk. We help businesses capture more calls and book more appointments with an AI call desk. I'll send a quick note as well — reach me at [phone]. Thanks!"

## Do / Don't
```txt
DO: identify yourself + company honestly · state a real reason for calling · offer a no-cost demo · respect their time · log the outcome.
DON'T: use a fake reason to call · claim guaranteed revenue/savings/compliance/staffing cuts · say they're "approved" before discovery ·
       record without disclosure/consent · keep contacting after an opt-out.
```
