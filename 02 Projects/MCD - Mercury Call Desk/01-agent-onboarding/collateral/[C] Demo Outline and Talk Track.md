# Demo Outline & Talk Track (~20 minutes)

For now, the company (Hamilton or a service rep) leads the demo while the partner supports and learns (see `14_DEMO_PROCESS_AND_HANDOFF.md`). This is the standard flow.

## Before the call (partner preps)
- Confirm attendees (decision-maker present), their business type, and the pain points from discovery.
- Share the CRM record + notes with the company demo lead so we can pre-train the sample assistant.
- Test the join link, audio, and the sample assistant.

## 1. Open & rapport (2 min)
- Introduce the team and the agenda: "We'll keep this to about 20 minutes — confirm a couple of things about how your calls work today, show you a sample assistant set up for a business like yours, and talk through what fit looks like."

## 2. Quick discovery confirm (3 min)
- "Before I show you anything — when a customer calls after hours or while you're busy, what happens today?"
- Confirm: call volume, after-hours handling, appointment needs, current tools, who decides.

## 3. Live sample demo (8 min)
- Play/walk through the **sample AI assistant** handling a call for their type of business: greeting, answering a common question, routing, and booking an appointment.
- Tie each moment back to their pain: "This is the after-hours call you mentioned — instead of voicemail, it captured the lead and booked the appointment."
- **Honesty guardrail:** present it as a **representative sample**. Do **not** claim it's live-connected to their systems or pulling their real data unless that's actually been configured. Say: "This is a sample of how it would work for you; we confirm the exact setup and any integrations during implementation." (Real API/live data is available once a client is set up.)

## 4. Fit & options (4 min)
- Recap which capabilities matter most to them.
- Outline the path: discovery → setup → go live. Mention plan options at a high level (retail; see `04_PRODUCT_CATALOG.md`). Escalate anything custom/regulated.

## 5. Close & next step (3 min)
- "Based on what you've seen, does this look like it would help with [their goal]?"
- Set the concrete next step: proposal / checkout / follow-up — and book the date.
- Partner: log the outcome, move the CRM stage, and create the next-step task.

## Objection quick-hits
- **"We already have a phone system."** → "Most businesses we talk to do. The question is whether it's consistently catching after-hours calls, routing, and follow-up the way you want. The demo shows whether there's a gap worth closing."
- **"Will it replace my staff?"** → "It's about coverage and speed — handling overflow and after-hours so your team focuses on higher-value work. We don't promise staffing changes."
- **"Is it compliant/secure for my industry?"** → "Great question — let me bring in our team to confirm specifics for your industry before we go further." (Escalate.)

## Do not
- Promise guaranteed revenue, savings, staffing cuts, compliance, or timelines.
- Represent a scripted sample as a live integration.
- Quote custom pricing or terms on the spot — escalate.
