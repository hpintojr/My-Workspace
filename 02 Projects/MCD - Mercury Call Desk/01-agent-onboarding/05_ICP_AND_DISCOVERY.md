# Ideal Customer Profile and Discovery Guide

## Core Ideal Customer Profile

The best prospects are businesses that:

- Depend on inbound calls, booked appointments, urgent requests, estimates, intake, reservations, or routing.
- Lose leads when calls go to voicemail or are answered slowly.
- Have limited staff coverage during busy periods, after hours, weekends, or lunch breaks.
- Want a more consistent first response without adding immediate front-desk headcount.
- Can identify a clear business impact from missed calls, delayed response, or manual call handling.

## Priority vertical examples

- Home services and field service businesses
- Legal offices and intake-heavy professional services
- Medical, wellness, and appointment-based practices where approved workflow and compliance review are completed
- Restaurants, hospitality, and reservation-based businesses
- Auto services, repair, towing, and dispatch-oriented businesses
- Property management, real estate, and leasing teams
- Financial services and other consultation-based businesses after management approval

## Discovery questions

Ask conversationally. Do not interrogate the prospect.

1. How are inbound calls handled today?
2. What happens when the team is busy or the office is closed?
3. Are missed calls usually voicemail, a callback list, or a lost opportunity?
4. What types of calls take up the most team time?
5. Do callers need appointments, estimates, directions, status updates, or general answers?
6. Which calls should be routed to a person right away?
7. Do you use a CRM, scheduling tool, dispatch system, or phone system today?
8. How many locations or teams would need to be involved?
9. Is the goal coverage, lead capture, appointment booking, routing, staff efficiency, or a mix?
10. Who else should join a demo before making a decision?
11. Is there a timeline or business event driving the need?

## Qualification signals

### Strong fit

- The prospect names a current call-handling problem.
- They can describe missed-call, staff-time, or appointment impact.
- The decision-maker participates or agrees to attend the demo.
- They have a real timeline and willingness to review a solution.

### Needs follow-up / nurture

- Interested but no urgency.
- Needs a partner, owner, or operations manager involved.
- Existing contract or budget timing delays a decision.

### Disqualify or escalate

- Prospect only wants a free tool with no business need.
- Prospect wants prohibited or unapproved use.
- Prospect asks for guarantees or commitments not supported by the company.
- Prospect is not an authorized decision-maker and cannot bring one into the process.
