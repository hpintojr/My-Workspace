# Compliance, Data Security, and Communication Rules

## 1. Core policy

All prospect and customer communications must be professional, accurate, respectful, documented in the CRM when meaningful, and conducted through approved MCD channels where required.

Partners independently control their schedules and prospecting approach, but may not make prohibited claims, misuse Company data, bypass DNC, change customer terms, or represent authority they do not have.

## 2. Approved communication model

- Personal social accounts and personal devices may be used for independent prospecting in good faith.
- Customer/prospect email must be sent through the MCD CRM/company-email system.
- MCD currently has no approved Partner phone or SMS program. Do not create, use, or represent an MCD calling/SMS workflow without written approval.
- Use approved brand assets, scripts, calendar links, published pricing, proposals, customer agreements, and email signatures.
- Identify yourself accurately as an MCD Account Executive, Sales Representative, or Sales Agent.
- Standard Partners may quote published pricing only. Only written Advanced Partner authority permits custom pricing above MCD's confidential floor. Discounts, waived setup fees, custom scope, or nonstandard terms always require manager approval before contract generation.

## 3. DNC and opt-outs — immediate action required

- Honor any “do not call,” “do not contact,” “unsubscribe,” “stop,” or comparable request immediately.
- Enter the DNC/opt-out in the CRM immediately, including channel, date/time, and any relevant notes.
- DNC means total blackout from calls, SMS, sales email, marketing email, and social DMs.
- Transactional billing, support, security, and system-outage messages may continue only where permitted.
- A Partner who receives an opt-out through a personal phone or social account is strictly responsible for immediate CRM logging and must not continue promotional contact.
- Do not assume B2B status eliminates compliance, privacy, disclosure, DNC, recording-consent, platform, or state-law requirements.

## 4. Required practices

- Be honest about why you are contacting the business and what MCD does.
- Present demos as representative samples. Do not say a demo is connected to a prospect's systems or data unless that integration is actually configured.
- Log meaningful activity, two-way contact, quote/pricing discussions, next steps, meetings, support matters, and escalation in the CRM.
- Escalate legal, privacy, data-security, regulatory, technical, custom-integration, custom-scope, pricing-exception, complaint, chargeback, or contract questions to management.
- Treat leads, customer data, recordings, internal pricing/costs, commission mechanics, scripts, vendor relationships, backend operations, and internal materials as confidential.
- Report any actual or suspected unauthorized storage, export, screenshot, disclosure, or misuse of Company information within 24 hours.
- Delete any local Company information within 24 hours after departure.

## 5. Prohibited practices

- Misrepresentation, deceptive urgency, false affiliation, or unapproved claims.
- Promising guaranteed income, revenue, savings, compliance, staffing reduction, service level, implementation timing, customer outcome, or reseller status.
- Saying an MCD Partner is an employee, franchisee, owner, or authorized signer unless that is true and authorized in writing.
- Unapproved bulk messaging, prerecorded messages, auto-dialing, call recording, SMS campaigns, or contact automation.
- Sharing internal costs, wholesale prices, commission splits, scripts, customer/lead data, vendor/platform relationships, credentials, recordings, or backend operations outside authorized systems.
- Asking prospects to transmit payment cards, credentials, government IDs, health data, or other sensitive data through ordinary notes, text, personal email, or social DMs.
- Continuing sales/marketing contact after DNC, opt-out, reassignment, House status, or another restriction.
- Exporting, downloading, screenshotting, copying, retaining, or using Company records outside approved systems.

## 6. Public identity and content

- Partners may publicly represent themselves in outbound business activity as an MCD Account Executive, Sales Representative, or Sales Agent.
- Partners may use MCD website and approved brand assets in good faith.
- Identifiable client names, logos, screenshots, testimonials, or case studies require written approval from both MCD and the client.
- Generic, anonymized examples/metrics may be used if accurate and not misleading.

## 7. Escalate immediately when

- A prospect requests nonstandard pricing, waived setup fees, custom scope, a contract change, a guarantee, a regulated-industry commitment, or special integration.
- A prospect/customer raises legal, TCPA, recording-consent, privacy, security, HIPAA, financial-regulation, data-retention, or compliance questions.
- A client reports a complaint, chargeback, fraud concern, security incident, threat, legal demand, or urgent service issue.
- You are unsure whether a statement, channel, or claim is permitted.

When uncertain, pause the promise, protect the client, document the issue, and escalate to MCD management.
