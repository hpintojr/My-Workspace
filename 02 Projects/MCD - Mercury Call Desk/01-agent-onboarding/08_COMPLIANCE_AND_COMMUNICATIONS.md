# Compliance, Data Security, and Communication Rules

## Company policy

All prospect and customer communications must be professional, accurate, respectful, and limited to company-approved channels and messaging.

## Required practices

- Use approved caller ID, email signature, text templates, calendar links, pricing, proposals, and contracts.
- Identify yourself and the company accurately.
- Honor “do not call,” “unsubscribe,” “stop,” or similar opt-out requests immediately and document them in the CRM.
- Use only approved recording disclosures and call-handling language.
- Escalate legal, medical, financial, technical, privacy, security, or compliance questions to management.
- Treat every lead, customer, call recording, proposal, and internal document as confidential company information.
- Use company systems only for lead communication and customer records.

## Prohibited practices

- Misrepresentation, false urgency, deceptive openers, or false affiliation claims.
- Unapproved bulk messaging, recorded calls, prerecorded messages, or auto-dialing.
- Promising guaranteed revenue, savings, compliance, staffing reductions, service levels, or implementation timing.
- Sharing internal prices, scripts, client lists, compensation details, credentials, recordings, or customer data outside authorized channels.
- Asking prospects to send payment cards, credentials, health data, government ID, or other sensitive data through ordinary texts or CRM notes.
- Continuing to contact a lead after an opt-out or restriction is recorded.

## Escalate immediately when

- A prospect asks about legal compliance, privacy, HIPAA, TCPA, recording consent, financial regulation, or data-security commitments.
- A prospect requests special pricing, a custom feature, a contract change, or a guarantee.
- A potential customer reports a complaint, security incident, chargeback, threat, or legal demand.
- You are unsure whether a claim is allowed.

When in doubt, pause the promise and ask management.
