# Confidentiality, Intellectual Property & Non-Compete Agreement (DRAFT)

> **Important:** Internal working draft built from the Sales Partner Launch Kit and master ops manual. **Not legal advice** and **not** ready to sign. A licensed attorney must review and finalize before use. **Non-compete and non-solicit clauses are heavily regulated and vary by state (some states limit or ban them, and the FTC has acted in this area)** — have counsel confirm enforceability and scope for each jurisdiction. Bracketed `[ ]` items are owner inputs.

This Confidentiality, Intellectual Property & Non-Compete Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of **[Effective Date]** between **[Legal Entity Name]** ("Company") and **[Sales Partner Name]** ("Recipient"). It supplements and is incorporated into the Sales Partner Agreement.

## 1. Confidential Information
1.1 "Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of Company, including: pricing, cost, and commission/gross-profit calculations; sales scripts, openers, objection handlers, and discovery frameworks; the Ideal Customer Profile and targeting; lead and customer lists and contact data; CRM data, pipelines, and configurations; call recordings; proposals and contracts; product, technical, and integration details; vendor/platform relationships (including Sulus.ai arrangements); business, marketing, and financial information; and any materials marked or reasonably understood to be confidential.
1.2 Confidential Information does not include information that is or becomes public through no fault of Recipient, was lawfully known before disclosure, or is independently developed without use of Confidential Information.

## 2. Obligations
2.1 Recipient will use Confidential Information solely to perform under the Sales Partner Agreement, and will not disclose, publish, forward, reproduce, or share it with anyone outside Company's authorized systems and personnel.
2.2 Recipient will protect Confidential Information with at least reasonable care and only access what is assigned to them.
2.3 Recipient will not store Confidential Information (including leads or customer data) on personal devices, spreadsheets, email, messaging apps, or any unapproved tool, and will not export or download it.
2.4 Recipient will report any suspected loss, unauthorized access, or disclosure to Company immediately.

## 3. Intellectual Property
3.1 All materials, scripts, processes, branding, software, content, data, leads, customer records, and derivatives provided by Company or created in connection with the engagement are and remain the **exclusive property of Company**.
3.2 To the extent Recipient contributes any ideas, edits, recordings, or work product related to Company's materials or customers, Recipient **assigns all rights** in such contributions to Company and will sign documents reasonably needed to perfect that assignment.
3.3 Recipient receives no license to Company IP except the limited, revocable right to use approved materials to perform during the engagement.

## 4. Non-Solicitation
4.1 **Customers/prospects:** during the engagement and for **[period, e.g., 12 months]** after it ends, Recipient will not solicit, divert, or do business with any Company customer or actively-worked prospect that Recipient learned of or worked through Company, for competing AI telephony / call-desk services.
4.2 **Personnel:** during the engagement and for **[period, e.g., 12 months]** after, Recipient will not solicit or recruit Company's partners, agents, employees, or contractors to leave Company.

## 5. Non-Compete (jurisdiction-dependent — confirm enforceability)
5.1 During the engagement and for **[3 months]** after it ends, Recipient will not, within **[defined territory / customer base]**, directly or indirectly own, operate, work for, or assist a business that provides **competing AI telephony or call-desk automation** services targeting the same customer base Recipient worked while engaged.
5.2 This restriction is intended to protect Confidential Information and goodwill and to be limited to what is reasonable and enforceable; if a court finds it overbroad, it will be enforced to the maximum extent permitted ("blue-pencil"). **Where prohibited or limited by law, this Section is modified or unenforced accordingly.**

## 6. Return of Materials
On termination or on request, Recipient will promptly return or permanently destroy all Confidential Information and Company materials in any form and certify compliance, and will lose all access to Company systems.

## 7. Remedies
Recipient agrees that breach of this Agreement would cause irreparable harm for which money damages are inadequate, and that Company is entitled to seek **injunctive and equitable relief** (without posting bond, where permitted) in addition to other remedies, including in court notwithstanding any arbitration clause.

## 8. Term and Survival
Confidentiality obligations continue for as long as the information remains confidential (and at minimum **[period, e.g., 3 years]** after termination); IP assignment is perpetual; restrictive covenants apply for the periods stated above.

## 9. General
9.1 Governed by the laws of the State of **[State]**, without regard to conflict-of-laws rules.
9.2 This Agreement supplements the Sales Partner Agreement; if they conflict on confidentiality, IP, or restrictive covenants, this Agreement controls.
9.3 Severability and no-waiver apply as in the Sales Partner Agreement.
