### Page-count note — resolve before final layout

The Book Overview lists 32 planned pages while the Character Bible carries an older 27-page interior assumption. For Book 1 development, this blueprint uses **32 pages**. Update the older 27-page note only after the final Lulu product configuration and author approval are captured.

## What the reference draft contributes

The reference draft establishes the emotional premise and the core content sequence:

1. Penny has mixed feelings about infusion day.
2. Nurse Ivy arrives and creates safety through calm explanations.
3. Benny is Penny's constant comfort companion.
4. The infusion is explained in child-safe language.
5. Penny prepares, receives care, and feels proud.
6. Polly, Danny, and Ellie make medical equipment understandable as friendly helpers.
7. The book ends with conversation prompts, activities, a certificate, and caregiver guidance.

## What will NOT carry over unchanged

- Letter-size pages, clipped artwork, white gaps, split images, and manual partial-page exports.
- Giant “Page 1 / Page 2” ribbons on every story page.
- Text baked into AI art.
- Dense combinations of story text, banners, callouts, charts, activity instructions, and character art on one page.
- Device-helper art that conflicts with the approved locks.
- Any step-by-step clinical instruction that could be read as universal medical guidance.

## Character and visual rules

- Penny is a 2–3-year-old toddler with fixed face, pigtails, mint bows, mint heart dress, and toddler proportions.
- Benny is a soft plush toy about Penny's torso size — never a realistic bear or a bear child.
- Nurse Ivy is warm, calm, and at Penny's eye level whenever possible.
- Penny may be nervous, but she is always safe, supported, and never in severe distress.
- Device helper focus: Polly = purple smartphone-shaped pump, no bow; Danny = purple dial/tubing helper, no bow; Ellie = cream round infusion ball, mint bow.
- Use only one helper as an active character focus per scene.
- No scary needles, blood, or hyper-realistic clinical visuals.
- Every meaningful medical moment includes at least one reassuring face.
- Text remains live, editable Canva text — never generated inside artwork.

## Layout rules

- No large decorative page-number ribbons in the story.
- Front matter has no visible numbering.
- Story pages may use quiet folios only after the final page-number system is approved.
- Keep all text, faces, and essential objects out of the center gutter for planned spreads.
- Establish recurring text zones, but do not force every page into the same layout.
- Use open picture-book pacing: some pages should carry only a few lines of text.
- Reserve high-detail information panels for activities/back matter, not emotional story moments.
- Build artwork with room for real text; never rely on AI-generated lettering.
