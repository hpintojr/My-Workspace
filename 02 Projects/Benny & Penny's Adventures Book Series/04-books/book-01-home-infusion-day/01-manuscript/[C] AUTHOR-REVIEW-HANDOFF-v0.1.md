# Book 1 — Author Review Handoff

## Purpose

Give Michelle a simple, page-by-page view of the complete Book 1 experience before any manuscript lock, illustration planning, Canva production, Lulu setup, or medical-content editing begins.

## Author-review packet

**Current review artifact:** `Benny_and_Penny_Book1_Author_Page_Expectations_Guide_v0.1.2.pdf`

This private review PDF is intentionally concise. It lists all 48 pages by title and explains what the reader should expect on each page without displaying full manuscript text, illustration instructions, production notes, or printer requirements.

The private PDF is not committed to GitHub. The supporting source and production record remain in this repository.

## What Michelle is approving now

For every page, Michelle may mark:

- **Keep** — the page belongs in the book as currently described.
- **Revise** — the page belongs, but its purpose, title, content, or sequence needs change.
- **Move** — the page belongs, but should be placed elsewhere.
- **Discuss** — the page needs a decision before it can move forward.

Current review scope:

1. The 48-page sequence.
2. The purpose and expected reader experience of each page.
3. The placement of Story, Child Education, Parent & Caregiver, and Activities & Keepsake sections.

Not yet under review:

- Final wording of every manuscript page.
- Final illustration composition or character poses.
- Canva page design.
- Lulu trim, spine, paperback, hardcover, or cover templates.
- Final medical-content language.

## Required approval gate

Do not advance to layout or illustration until Michelle’s page-level decisions are captured and the 48-page sequence is confirmed.

## Workflow after Michelle’s approval

1. Capture every Keep / Revise / Move / Discuss decision in a revision log.
2. Create **Book 1 Author Manuscript v1.0** — a clean reading document containing final page text in order, without illustrator or production overload.
3. Obtain qualified medical-accuracy review for IV, catheter, medicine-delivery, caregiver, and disclaimer language.
4. Create the 48-page production map with final text, page purpose, illustration moment, character/prop requirements, and activity layout guidance.
5. Produce a small set of visual proof pages for Michelle’s approval before full illustration and Canva layout.
6. After text and visual approval, complete Canva interior layout, capture live Lulu specifications, build separate paperback and hardcover wraps, then run proof and release workflows.
